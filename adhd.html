ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder affecting attention, impulsivity, and activity levels, often starting in childhood but continuing into adulthood.

Causes

ADHD can be caused by:

  • Genetics: Family history plays a significant role in the development of ADHD.
  • Brain Structure: Differences in brain development may affect impulse control and attention span.
  • Environmental Factors: Prenatal exposure to alcohol, tobacco, or toxins may increase the risk.

Signs

Signs of ADHD include:

  • Difficulty focusing on tasks or following instructions.
  • Frequent fidgeting or restlessness.
  • Impulsivity and acting without thinking of consequences.
  • Problems with organization and time management.

Prevention

Managing ADHD involves:

  • Establishing clear routines and structures to stay organized.
  • Using positive reinforcement and setting realistic goals.
  • Engaging in activities that require focus and patience, such as puzzles or games.
