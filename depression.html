Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that involves persistent sadness, hopelessness, and a lack of interest in activities once enjoyed.

Causes

Depression can be caused by:

  • Genetic Factors: A family history of depression increases the likelihood of developing the disorder.
  • Biological Differences: Changes in brain structure or function may contribute to depression.
  • Trauma and Stress: Major life events such as loss or trauma can trigger depressive episodes.

Signs

Symptoms of depression include:

  • Persistent sadness or feelings of emptiness.
  • Loss of interest in hobbies or activities.
  • Changes in appetite or sleep patterns.
  • Feelings of hopelessness, worthlessness, or guilt.

Prevention

Preventing depression involves:

  • Maintaining strong social connections and support networks.
  • Engaging in physical exercise to boost mood.
  • Developing healthy sleep routines.
  • Early intervention and seeking professional help when needed.
