Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are severe mental health conditions that affect a personâ€™s eating behaviors and are often linked to distorted body image.

Causes

Causes of eating disorders include:

  • Genetic Factors: A family history of eating disorders or mental illness.
  • Societal Pressures: Cultural emphasis on thinness and body image can influence behavior.
  • Psychological Factors: Emotional distress, low self-esteem, or trauma can contribute.

Signs

Signs of eating disorders include:

  • Restricting food intake or binge eating.
  • Obsessive thoughts about weight, food, or body image.
  • Rapid weight loss or gain.
  • Unhealthy preoccupation with dieting or exercise.

Prevention

Preventing eating disorders involves:

  • Promoting body positivity and self-acceptance.
  • Encouraging balanced, healthy relationships with food.
  • Raising awareness about the dangers of dieting culture.
