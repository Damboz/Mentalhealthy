Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are mental health conditions characterized by excessive fear or worry. These feelings can disrupt daily functioning and affect academic and personal lives.

Causes

Common causes of anxiety disorders include:

  • Genetics: Anxiety can run in families, indicating a possible genetic predisposition.
  • Brain Chemistry: Imbalances in neurotransmitters like serotonin can trigger anxiety.
  • Stress: Academic, social, or personal stress can contribute to developing anxiety disorders.
  • Trauma: Experiencing trauma, such as abuse or loss, may increase the risk of anxiety.

Signs

Signs of anxiety disorders include:

  • Constant worry or fear about everyday situations.
  • Difficulty concentrating or feeling tense.
  • Rapid heartbeat or shortness of breath.
  • Restlessness and trouble sleeping.

Prevention

To prevent or manage anxiety, try these strategies:

  • Regular physical activity like jogging or yoga to reduce stress.
  • Practice mindfulness techniques such as meditation or deep breathing.
  • Develop time management skills to balance academics and personal time.
  • Seek professional help early if anxiety symptoms arise.
